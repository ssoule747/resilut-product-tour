Module 8 — Wholesaler Deal Intake

Wholesaler Deal Intake

Wholesalers submit deals in messy, inconsistent formats. Before underwriting can run numbers, someone has to normalize and validate everything. This is slow and manual.

What Happens in Real Life

A wholesaler finds a beat-up house, puts it under contract, and submits through an online portal — photos, descriptions, partial data. It’s messy. Maybe they forgot the square footage. Maybe the photos are blurry. Maybe the ARV is wildly optimistic. Someone on the team has to manually chase down the missing info, normalize the formatting, and decide if it’s even worth looking at.

What the AI Does

  • Reads everything submitted — text, photos, partial data — and cleans it all up into a normalized record

  • Classifies the property — type, condition tier (light / medium / heavy / gut), market segment

  • Detects missing data — auto-generates follow-up requests to the wholesaler for incomplete fields

  • Scores deal quality — based on completeness, property attributes, and wholesaler track record

  • Maintains a wholesaler reliability index — tracks ARV accuracy, close rates, and cost deviations over time

Station 02
Module 1 — Inspection & Defect Intelligence

Inspection & Defect Intelligence

Inspection reports arrive as unstructured PDFs, photos, and free-text findings. These need to become structured, actionable inputs for renovation scoping.

What Happens in Real Life

An inspector walks through the property, takes photos of every defect, writes notes by hand or on a tablet, and produces a messy 30-page PDF full of paragraphs, inconsistent terminology, and scattered photos. Some inspectors use Inspectify, others email Word docs. The team has to manually read each one, pull out what matters, and reformat it into something the renovation team can use.

What the AI Does

  • Ingests the entire PDF — plus all photos and Inspectify API data into a single processing pipeline

  • Breaks everything into a structured defect ledger — table rows instead of paragraphs, one row per defect

  • Tags each defect — System, Trade, Severity, Urgency, Code Relevance — using a consistent taxonomy

  • Classifies and tags photos — “cracked foundation,” “mold,” “damaged roof” — linked to their defect rows

  • Generates concise summaries — standardized from verbose inspector notes so every report reads the same

Station 03
Module 2 — Renovation Scoping

Renovation Scoping

Translating inspection defects into an itemized, costed scope of work is the #1 bottleneck. This is the slowest step and directly constrains deal velocity.

What Happens in Real Life

Someone takes the defect list from the inspection and manually builds a renovation plan — what work needs to happen, who does it, how much it costs, how long it takes. They cross-reference cost databases, call contractors for quotes, and piece together a scope of work document. Currently painfully slow and the single biggest constraint on deal velocity.

What the AI Does

  • Maps each defect to line items — specific renovation tasks with quantities, trade assignments, and work descriptions

  • Cross-references RSMeans pricing — with regional cost adjustments. AI interprets, code calculates (lookups, multipliers, totals)

  • Generates contractor-ready SOW documents — complete scope of work files ready to send to contractors for bidding

  • Analyzes change orders — reasonable? Cost impact? Timeline effect? Compares against original scope

  • Drafts contractor communications — scope clarifications, change order responses, and progress inquiries

Module 3 — Underwriting Intelligence

Underwriting Intelligence

Every deal needs a go/no-go decision based on ARV, renovation cost, rental income, expenses, financing, and risk. Speed and accuracy determine competitive positioning.

What Happens in Real Life

Someone gathers all the numbers from multiple sources — ARV estimates, renovation budgets, rental comps, insurance costs, tax records, financing terms — and plugs them into a spreadsheet. They make a judgment call: can we make money on this deal? How much risk is there? What’s the maximum we should pay? This requires experience, and mistakes are expensive.

What the AI Does

  • Pulls ARV from HouseCanary + Quantarium — cross-validated. When sources agree, confidence is high. When they spread, it flags the gap.

  • Renovation cost from Station 3 — itemized scope with contingencies already built in

  • Rental income from Rentometer + HouseCanary — comp-based projections with market context

  • Historical deal data via RAG — “what happened the last 50 times we bought a house like this?”

  • Generates confidence scores — agreement between sources = high. Spread = low. Quantified uncertainty.

  • Sensitivity analysis in plain English — “If rents drop 10%, deal still works. If renovation exceeds 15%, deal goes negative.”

  • Anomaly detection — flags weird deals compared to comps. Something doesn’t look right? You’ll know.

  • Calculates max acquisition price — and counteroffer ranges based on target returns

Module 6 — Refinance Intelligence
💰 This is the “Repeat” in BRRRR — Capital Recycling

Refinance Intelligence

Identify when assets are refi-ready, assemble lender packages, compare term sheets — all at speed to recycle capital and fund the next deal.

What Happens in Real Life

After 6–12 months of stable tenancy, refinance the property to pull out the invested capital. Use that money to buy the next house. This is the engine of scale — the faster you refi, the faster you grow. But assembling packages, comparing lenders, and timing the refi all take effort.

What the AI Does

  • Monitors and flags refi-readiness — DSCR, occupancy, seasoning thresholds met automatically

  • Assembles lender packages — SOW, CapEx summary, photos, lease, rent roll — quality-checked

  • Compares term sheets — side by side from multiple lenders, flags unfavorable terms

  • Predicts appraisal value — and cash-out amount based on historical refi data
